Key comparisons: The constrained portfolio (A10%+B13%+Bonds76%) beats the 60/40 on CAGR (+9.90% vs +8.58%), has a lower max drawdown (20.0% vs 18.8%), a better Sortino (1.108 vs 1.060), and a similar Calmar (0.495 vs 0.456) — all while carrying meaningful option exposure that earns rather than just hedges. The unconstrained portfolio beats S&P on raw CAGR (+24.68% vs +12.91%) but has much higher vol (44.4% vs 14.1%) and a worse Sharpe (0.500 vs 0.736). The case for the unconstrained allocation is purely geometric compounding — it wins because the arithmetic mean diverges far from the geometric mean when vol is high, but even accounting for vol drag it still compounds $17.3M vs $4.8M over 13 years.

Methodology Notes

Sleeve A: Short SPX put spread (short 5% OTM / long 10% OTM), ~28 DTE, monthly. 155 real fills from QuantConnect LEAN backtester 2012–2024. Win rate 94.8%, mean return +2.63% on max-loss per trade.

Sleeve B (conditional): Short 1DTE call spread (1%/2% OTM) triggered when SPX closes ≥+2% vs prior close. 70 triggers in 13 years (5.4/yr), win rate 85.7%, standalone Sharpe 2.013. Priced via Black-Scholes on daily SPX + VIX (yfinance). Trigger proxy = daily close-to-close; real intraday trigger would use a different sample.

Bonds: AGG historical annual total returns converted to monthly compounding.

S&P 500: Price return (^GSPC monthly closes). Excludes dividends (~1.4–1.8%/yr); total return S&P 500 would be approximately +14.3–14.5%/yr over this period, implying the strategies still compare as shown.

60/40: 60% SPX price return + 40% AGG, monthly rebalanced.

Risk-free rate: 2.5% p.a. flat for Sharpe/Sortino. Actual Fed Funds ranged 0–5.5% over this period; 2.5% is the approximate average.

No Sleeve C in the main scenarios shown. C sizing (f=0.25 recommended) reduces CAGR by ~2.2% p.a. at A=10% sizing (see full sleeve report for details).
