The other 154 months it expires worthless, costing −9% of A max_loss each. Net cumulative: −1,081% of A max_loss over 13 years. The drag cannot be overcome by one extraordinary payoff.

Why C stays in the book anyway: Sleeve C is policy, not alpha — it is the tail risk guard required by the v1 spec ("Sleeve C roll is sacred"). The optimization correctly says it destroys return-on-capital. The decision is whether the crash protection justifies the drag. In COVID March 2020, C paid +7.6% of total capital in a single month (at fa=10%, fc=0.25) — partially offsetting A's −9.5% that month. The question is not "does C make money" (it doesn't) but "does Francisco require that tail protection regardless of cost."

Key Findings

① The unconstrained allocation (A60%+B30%+Bonds10%, C=0) wins every horizon. At 5yr it produces $5.1M vs $2.2M for the constrained version. At 13yr: $17.3M vs $3.4M. The gap widens with time because of compounding — a strategy that earns 24.68%/yr doubles every 3.2 years vs 11.4 years at 9.90%/yr.

② The cost: a 60% drawdown from peak. In March 2020, this portfolio dropped $4.5M in one month (from $7.6M peak to $3.0M trough). By November 2020 it had recovered most of it. In 2014-2015, it went from $1.30M to $928K — below the starting level. You need to hold through this without de-risking or margin calls, or the compounding advantage evaporates.

③ Conditional Sleeve B (mean-reversion) is the real alpha. Without B, A-only at 30% gives $2.82M over 13yr. With B at 30% capital allocation, the unconstrained portfolio gives $17.28M. The B contribution is especially powerful in high-volatility regimes (2020: +141.6% annual, 2022: near-flat instead of deep losses). B Sharpe standalone = 2.013 — it earns when markets are volatile and reverting, which is exactly when A struggles.

④ Sleeve C is a return destroyer at every sizing. Even at the "break-even carry" level (fc=0.34), C turns a $2.82M A-only outcome into a $1.00M zero-real-return outcome over 13 years. The one COVID payoff (+305% of A max_loss in Feb 2020) is not enough to compensate 154 months of premium drag. C remains in the book as a policy requirement, not as alpha.

⑤ Suggested paper-first path: Start at the constrained allocation (A10%+B13%+Bonds76%, C=0) to build confidence and prove the system works — this generates +9.90%/yr with MaxDD 20% and worst month −9.66%. After 12 months of paper validation, evaluate ramping toward the unconstrained target. The 13yr difference between these two choices is $17.3M vs $3.4M on $1M starting capital — the ramp decision is worth $14M on that figure.
